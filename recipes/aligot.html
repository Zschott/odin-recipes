Aligot

Aligot is a super cheesy mashed potato recipe that did the media rounds a few years back. Fair warning, this does involve folding in some cheese. How do you do that you may ask? Well, I cannot show you everything, David.

The Story

This is another recipe I made for my work's social media during the pandemic. At this point, I'd been selected to be a "social media champion", meaning that I had one day during the week where I had the keys to all our social media platforms and was responsible for making some kind of post. I wanted to start experimenting with the Reels feature on instagram, and after the successs of our Ricotta Pasta reel, I selected this for my second project.

This super stretchy & cheesy dish is best served fresh out of the pot for maximum stretch. As it looses heat, the aligot tends to firm up. Enjoy!

Serves 6-8

The Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2.5 lbs Yukon gold potatoes
  • 6 Tbsp butter
  • 1-2 cups heavy cream
  • ¾ lbs Fior di Pizza, shredded
  • 3/4 lbs Smoked Mozzarella, shredded
  • Salt, pepper, and olive oil to taste
  • Brenda’s Pepper Jelly for plating
  • Protein from Rettland Farm

Instructions

  1. Set butter, cream, and cheeses out to temper for at least 30 minutes to bring up to around room temperature.

  2. Skin and cube potatoes. Place in a pot with cold, salted water and bring to a boil. Drain the potatoes when tender (a knife or fork should stab into them with little resistance). Return to pot, and mash (or use a ricer) until smooth and no large chunks remain. Return to stove on a low heat.

  3. While continuously stirring, mix in butter, and slowly mix in 1 cup heavy cream.

  4. Fold in half of the cheese (just fold it in, David). Make sure the potatoes are smooth and homogeneous before adding the rest of the cheese. Add more heavy cream for a smoother texture. Taste for seasoning, adding salt, pepper and/or olive oil.

  5. Plate with your preferred protein (We recommend something from Retland Farms!) and a smear or hearty spoonful of Brenda’s Pepper Jelly. Enjoy!

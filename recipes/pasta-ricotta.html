Ricotta Pasta

Remember that viral feta pasta recipe from TikTok a while back? As it turns out, it can also be made with ricotta.

The Story

Alright, so I'm seriously cheating on this one. While I've made and written up everything else on this site so far, none of them were full on written up recipes; until now.

This recipe is one that ended up being so good that we did a full recipe for on my work's website. So really, I'm copying and pasting a webpage that already exists. Regardless, I was surprised at how well this one came out, and hope you enjoy!

Serves 4 - 5

The Recipe

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 medium red onion, roughly chopped
  • 3 whole cloves of garlic, peeled
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 basket of Caputo Brothers Creamery Ricotta
  • 2 Tbsp Salumeria Biellese Nduja
  • 1/2 cup basil, loosely packed
  • Wine for optional deglazing (or just to drink)
  • 1 lb. (450g) rigatoni or your choice of pasta
  • Your favorite topping cheese (Parmigiano Reggiano, pecorino, ricotta salata, etc.)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400Â° F

  2. Combine tomatoes, red onion, garlic, olive oil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper in a cooking dish. Make a sort of nest in the middle and place the full form of ricotta there. Bake for 45 minutes or until vegetables gain a dark color, and ricotta turns golden brown along the edges. Turn the dish halfway through.

  3. With 15 minutes left, add the Nduja and return to the oven to finish cooking. Additionally, this is a good spot to get your pasta boiling. Save a half cup of the pasta water!

  4. After cooking, remove the vegetable/ricotta pot from the oven. Carefully take out the ricotta and set aside, keeping it in one piece. Also remove the cloves of garlic, mash into a paste, and return to the pot. Add loosely torn basil to the vegetables.

  5. (Optional step) Add a splash of table wine (white or red), deglaze the pot, and place in oven for 5 - 10 minutes to remove alcohol.

  6. Add cooked pasta to the vegetables and mix with pasta water and your preferred topping cheese until nice and saucy.

  7. Transfer pasta to a serving dish. Top with ricotta, finely chopped basil, olive oil, freshly ground pepper, and more cheese to your liking. Buon Appetito!
